A computer scientist is a professional who studies and applies the principles of computing.
They explore how computers work, design algorithms, and develop new technologies.

Software Development

• The process of designing, coding, testing, and maintaining computer programs.
• Involves creating applications, websites, or systems that solve specific problems or provide services.

Computer Repair & Maintenance

• Fixing hardware or software issues in computers.
• Includes preventive care like cleaning, updating software, and replacing faulty components to keep systems running smoothly.

Computer Networking

• Connecting computers and devices to share resources and information.
• Examples: Local Area Networks (LAN), Wide Area Networks (WAN), camera installation and the internet.

Embedded Systems & Robotics

• IoT: Devices connected to the internet that collect and share data (e.g., smart home gadgets, sensors).
• Embedded Systems: Specialized computers built into devices to perform dedicated functions (e.g., microcontrollers in cars, medical devices).

System Administration

• Managing and maintaining computer systems and servers.
• Tasks include user account management, security updates, backups, and ensuring systems run efficiently.

E-Civil Engineering

Civil engineering enhanced by software, simulations, and electronic systems to design, analyze, and manage infrastructure projects.
